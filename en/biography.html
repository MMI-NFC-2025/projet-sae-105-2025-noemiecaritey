Katherine Johnson, a woman who managed to put Man on the Moon

One small step for Man but a giant leap for women.

Katherine Johnson born as Creola Katherine Coleman in 1918 is an African-American mathematician and space engineer who actively participated in the Apollo 11 mission.

After becoming the third Black student in West Virginia, she completed brilliant studies in mathematics and French with the highest distinction.

An assignment
that changes everything

She continued her career in teaching then found a position at NACA, the former name of NASA. Upon her arrival, she was assigned with other women to mathematical calculations for aircraft black boxes.

She was temporarily assigned to help the male research team on flights. It was at this moment that she was noticed and became indispensable to the team.

What she deserves

She then participated in the Apollo 11 mission, one of the first space conquests.
Thanks to her calculations, she enabled the success of the mission by calculating the space rendezvous between the two modules but also by calculating the launch window for the rockets.

Katherine Johnson verified all the computer calculations so that man could take the first step on the Moon.
