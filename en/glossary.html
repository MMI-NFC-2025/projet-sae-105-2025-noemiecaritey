Glossary

DNA:
Acid from the nucleus of living cells, an essential component of chromosomes and carrier of genetic characteristics.
Trajectory Calculation:
Trajectory calculation is when we seek to know where an object will pass and where it will arrive after leaving a location.
Y Chromosome:
It is the male sex chromosome.
Nuclear Fission:
Fission is the splitting of a heavy atom into lighter atoms during the collision between an atom and a neutron.
NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration):
It is the federal agency responsible for most of the United States civilian space program
Particle Physics:
It is the branch of physics that studies the elementary constituents of matter and radiation, as well as their interactions.
Computer Program:
It is a set of instructions and operations intended to be executed by a computer.
Pulsars:
These small, very dense dead stars emit electromagnetic radiation in the form of beams that sweep through space at regular intervals, like cosmic lighthouses.
Space Rendezvous:
It is an organized meeting in space between spacecraft, or between a spacecraft and a celestial object at zero or very low relative speed.
Cubic Root:
A mathematical notion concerning numbers, which is equal to its real cubic root multiplied twice by itself.
Rehabilitation:
To restore to one's rights, to restore the esteem and consideration that had been lost.
Shell Structure of the Atomic Nucleus:
In an atom, electrons are distributed in shells around the nucleus, with successive shells being increasingly distant from the nucleus.
